# BRAHMO Clinical Governance Architecture

This document outlines the technical design, architectural patterns, and engineering decisions implemented in the **BRAHMO Governance Engine**.

---

## 1. Bounded BFS Invalidation Cascade

### Bounding Bounded Traversal
Guideline linkages in clinical environments can form deeply nested, complex dependency graphs. If a root-level constraint changes, propagating that invalidation downstream recursively without constraints poses two major risks:
1. **Clinical Fatigue / Unbounded Staleness**: Flagging hundreds of distantly related, minor procedures or facts as "stale" disrupts hospital operations and causes notification fatigue.
2. **Infinite Loops / Stack Overflow**: Cyclic or multi-parent references in dependency edges can lead to infinite loops.

### The BFS Approach
We implement a **bounded Breadth-First Search (BFS)** traversal:
- **`DERIVED_FROM` Only**: The cascade walks only `DERIVED_FROM` edges. Edges like `SUPPORTS`, `REQUIRES`, or `CONTRADICTS` represent association or dependencies, but updating a parent node does not automatically invalidate a supporting facts node.
- **Max Depth Limit (`max_depth = 3`)**: Traversal stops strictly at a depth of 3 levels downstream from the root trigger. This ensures clinical alerts remain focused on immediate and secondary derivatives.
- **Visited Set Guard**: A standard `Set` collects processed node IDs. If a node is multi-parent (e.g. derived from both Medicine and Orthopaedics protocols), it is visited exactly once, preventing infinite traversal.
- **State Preservation**: Already `SUPERSEDED` nodes are skipped. Already `REVIEW_REQUIRED` nodes are logged but require no duplicate status changes.

### Compliance Preservation (Legal Holds)
Clinical safety dictates that guidelines under active medical litigation must remain absolutely frozen:
- **`LEGAL_HOLD` Status**: A node set to `LEGAL_HOLD` is treated as immutable.
- **Cascade Bypassing**: When the BFS encounters a `LEGAL_HOLD` node, it halts further invalidation of that node and its downstream branches.
- **CASCADE_SKIP Event**: The skip is logged explicitly in the audit trail as a `CASCADE_SKIP` event with the explanation: `Node N-HELD skipped during cascade: LEGAL_HOLD status prevents status change.` This ensures full audit compliance and visibility without compromising frozen records.

## 2. Real-Time Vitals Health Scoring

The health score represents the overall clinical hygiene of a department's guidelines in real time across four distinct, deterministic dimensions:

$$\text{Overall Score} = 0.25 \times \text{Coverage} + 0.30 \times \text{Freshness} + 0.20 \times \text{Balance} + 0.25 \times \text{Consistency}$$

### The Four Dimensions
1. **Coverage (25%)**: Percentage of organizational hierarchy levels containing at least one `ACTIVE` node. Evaluates whether clinical guidelines span all critical departments/levels.
2. **Freshness (30%)**: Percentage of active guidelines that are not review-required and have not passed their `valid_until` decay window.
3. **Balance (20%)**: Standard deviation across node type categories (`FACT`, `DECISION`, `CONSTRAINT`, `ANTI_PATTERN`). Higher balance indicates a healthy, well-rounded guideline mix.
4. **Consistency (25%)**: Ratio of active nodes not flagged for review (meaning no stale dependencies).

### Deferred vs. Immediate Recomputation
- **The Problem**: Running full recomputations immediately during an invalidation cascade causes the health score to plummet instantly (e.g. from 86% to 74% or lower) due to stale review flags. This results in unnecessary panic among leadership and visual alarm on the dashboard before reviewers have had a chance to confirm validity.
- **The Solution**: During a cascade trigger, the engine updates the department's health score state to `pending = true`, displaying a `⚠️ Review Pending` banner on the dashboard. Immediate scoring recalculations are **deferred**.
- **Recompute Triggers**: Vitals are fully recalculated under two circumstances:
  1. A user manually clicks the **Recompute** button on the dashboard.
  2. A clinician completes a human review, marking an affected node as `ACTIVE` again or replacing it via a `SUPERSEDED` action. This causes the score to gradually recover.

## 3. Pulse Notification Routing

Alerts generated by clinical events must be directed to individuals who have the authority and context to act on them:

- **Targeted routing**: The engine queries:
  $$\text{Affected Node} \rightarrow \text{Department} \rightarrow \text{Users in Department}$$
- **Role Isolation**: Alerts are routed strictly to users with `HOD`, `DOCTOR`, or `EDITOR` roles. Admins and generic viewers are excluded from routine clinical alerts.
- **Departmental Boundaries**: If a cascade affects a Gen Medicine node, only medicine physicians are alerted. Orthopaedics surgeons are kept completely isolated, preventing notification noise.
- **Actionable Links**: Every Pulse alert carries a direct relative path (e.g. `/nodes/N-DRV-01`) allowing clinicians to immediately inspect the affected node on the details panel.
